Nutrition in plant

Nutrition:- Way of taking food or preparing food by an organism.

Two types of Nutrition are:-

Autotrophic mode of Nutrition:-

food prepared by an organism using simple inorganic compnents

Green plants,green algae and blue green algae(Cynabacteria) and certain bacteria prepare there food with in their body.
Green plants and algae prepare there food by the process of photosynthesis.
Green plants take:-
  1. CO2 from air.
  1. Water(H2O) from soil throuth roots
  1. Chorophyll(Green pigment present in leaves)
  1. Energy(from sunlight) and then produce food(Glucose/C6H12O6)

How does CO2 enters into leaves?

At upper and lower surface of leaves have very small pores in them which are called stomata

How does water reaches to leaves?

Water rises through a pulling force called transpirational pull caused due to less of water from leaves.

transpiration:- loss of water through leaves in plants.

*This loss of water also takes through stomata.
*Plants have cell organelle(Component of cells)in them called chloroplast.
*chloroplast traps solar energy (from sunlight) Chlorophyll.

Heterotrophic mode of Nutrition:-

food taken by organisms from other source

Organisms

What they do?

Parasite

These live in or out of another organisms body called host.

Sapropyhte

These live on dead and decaying organic matter eg. animal wastes,Human wastes,Plant and agricultural waste. Sapropyhtes first release same chemical from their body and break down into simpler form(digestion) and then into take release energy.

Insectivores

Some plants require certain nutrient from other source which are not available in soil or can't be transported through roots eg. Proteins,extra nitrogen. These insectivores trap the mosquetoes/flies and intake and digest to get more nutrients.

Symbiotic Relationship/Symbiosis

Two organisms live together and benefit each other without harming. eg.Lichens:- It is Symbiotic Relationship of Algae and fungi. In which algae provides food to fungi and fungi provides shelter to algae.
